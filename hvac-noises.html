Identify Your Noise
🔴 Act Now
Banging & Popping Noises From Your HVAC
A loud bang at furnace startup is delayed ignition — unburned gas igniting all at once. Banging from ducts is usually thermal expansion or a static pressure problem.

⚠ Schedule Service
Screeching & Squealing From Your HVAC
High-pitched squealing almost always means bearing wear in a blower or condenser fan motor. It's a leading indicator — acting on the squeal is far cheaper than what follows.
8 min read→
⚠ Check Pattern
Clicking, Ticking & Tapping From Your HVAC
A single click at startup is normal relay operation. Rapid or continuous clicking during a run cycle points to a failing contactor, weak capacitor, or control board problem.
8 min read→
⚠ Watch Closely
Buzzing & Humming From Your HVAC
A low hum from an AC condenser is often a capacitor struggling to start a motor. A hum from the air handler can be transformer buzz or relay chatter. Here's how to tell the difference.

Rattling, Vibrating & Shaking From Your HVAC
Rattling from ductwork, registers, or the equipment itself usually means a loose panel, debris in the system, or sheet metal flexing under changing air pressure.
