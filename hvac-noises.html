HVAC Noises Explained

Every HVAC noise is a diagnostic signal. The character of the sound — banging, grinding, squealing, clicking — tells you which of four physical systems is involved and how urgently you need to respond. Here's how to read each one.

HVAC Noise Diagnostic Guides

Homeowners judge HVAC noise by volume. Technicians judge it by character and pattern. Find the sound that matches yours — the guide will tell you what's causing it and whether to act now or schedule service.

🔴 Act Now
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Banging Noise
A loud bang at furnace startup is delayed ignition — unburned gas igniting all at once. A banging AC condenser usually means a loose or broken part inside the unit. Both require immediate attention.
8 min read →
⚠ Schedule Service
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Squealing Noise
Squealing from an HVAC system almost always means bearing wear in a blower motor, condenser fan motor, or belt-drive assembly. It's a leading indicator — acting on the squeal is dramatically cheaper than what comes next.
8 min read →
🔴 Shut Down
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Grinding Noise
Grinding is a lagging indicator — damage is actively occurring. Metal-on-metal grinding in a blower or condenser motor means bearing failure has progressed to the point of component destruction. Shut the system down.
8 min read →
⚠ Diagnose Pattern
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Clicking Noise
A single click at startup and shutdown is normal relay operation. Rapid or continuous clicking during a run cycle points to a failing contactor, a weak capacitor, or a control board problem. The pattern tells you which.
8 min read →
⚠ Watch Closely
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Humming Noise
A low hum from an AC condenser is often a capacitor struggling to start the compressor or fan motor. A hum from the air handler can be transformer or relay buzz. Here's how to tell the difference.
8 min read →
HVAC Noises
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Rattling Noise
Rattling from ductwork, registers, or the equipment itself usually means a loose panel, debris in the system, or sheet metal expanding and contracting. Here's how to locate and eliminate the source.

HVAC Noises
Why Your Ducts Are Making a Popping Noise
Popping or booming from ductwork at startup is almost always thermal expansion — sheet metal flexing as air pressure and temperature change. In most cases it's harmless, but chronic popping can indicate a static pressure problem.
7 min read →
🔴 Check Immediately
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Hissing Noise
Hissing from an AC system can mean refrigerant escaping through a leak — which requires immediate professional service. Hissing from ductwork is usually an air leak at a joint or connection. Here's how to tell which you have.
8 min read →
⚠ System Stress
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Whistling Noise
Whistling from registers or the return air grille almost always means the system is starved for air — a clogged filter, a closed register, or an undersized return is forcing air through a too-small opening.
7 min read →
HVAC Noises
Why Your HVAC Is Making a Gurgling Noise
Gurgling from an AC system usually means water is pooling in the condensate drain pan or drain line — and the system is trying to pull air through liquid. It can also indicate refrigerant issues in heat pumps.
