Why expert authorship matters

The internet is full of home repair advice written by people who have never held a pipe wrench, diagnosed an electrical fault, or stood in a basement watching a foundation crack grow. Most of it is technically accurate enough to avoid lawsuits but practically useless when you're standing in front of an actual problem.

Every article on this site is developed with the direct input of the professionals below. Their job is to make sure the diagnostic steps are real, the warnings are genuine, and the "call a pro" guidance reflects what they would actually tell a neighbor.

🔒

A note on privacy — why we use initials

Our contributors are active professionals with businesses, licenses, and reputations to protect. In an era of online harassment and privacy concerns, they've chosen to contribute under their initials rather than full names. This is a deliberate decision we respect and support. Their credentials, experience levels, and trade specializations are fully accurate and verifiable through their respective licensing boards.
